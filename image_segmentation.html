Image Segmentation using ResNet

  • by:Bhoomika Mathakala
  • date:September 2022

This project focuses on using Residual Neural Networks (ResNets) for image segmentation, aiming to divide digital images into meaningful segments. We implemented the ResNet50 architecture to improve training efficiency and performance. Using the Pascal VOC dataset, we trained and evaluated our model. Although our model performed well on simpler images, it struggled with more complex ones and did not match the performance of state-of-the-art models. The project demonstrates the potential of ResNets for image segmentation tasks, though it requires extensive training and optimization.

Introduction to Image Segmentation

Definition: Image segmentation separates a digital image into subsets or segments.

Purpose: Simplifies the image for easier processing and analysis.

Types: Edge-Based Segmentation Threshold-Based Segmentation Region-Based Segmentation Cluster-Based Segmentation Watershed Segmentation

Introduction

Problem Addressed: Difficulty in training deeper neural networks due to vanishing gradients and accuracy degradation.

Solution: Residual Neural Networks use skip connections to bypass some layers, which helps in effective training of deep networks.

Methodology: ResNet50 Architecture: 50 layers including 48 convolutional layers, one max pool layer, and one average pool layer. Uses bottleneck residual blocks to reduce parameter multiplication and speed up training.

Dataset: Pascal VOC dataset with images and corresponding segmentation masks.

Pre-processing: Conversion to RGB, resizing to 224x224 pixels, normalization, and conversion to torch tensors.

Training and Evaluation

Metrics: Validation loss, validation pixel accuracy, and validation mean IoU. Training Setup: Using Adam optimizer, CrossEntropyLoss, batch size of 50, learning rate of 0.1, and 12 epochs.

Results: Model performs well on simple images but struggles with complex ones.

Conclusion

Effectiveness: ResNets are effective for segmentation tasks but require extensive training.

Applications: Useful in various fields such as medical imaging and object detection.

Current Use: ResNets are used in pulmonary disease classification and cardiac MR scans.
