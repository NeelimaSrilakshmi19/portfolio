Emotion recognition with Deep Learning

  • by:Neelima Bollempalli
Skin cancer is the most common cancer in the United States. Some people are at higher risk of skin cancer than others, but anyone can get it. The most preventable cause of skin cancer is overexposure to ultraviolet (UV) light, either from the sun or from artificial sources like tanning beds. Current imaging techniques provide meticulous anatomical delineation and are the principal tools for establishing that neurological symptoms are the consequence of a brain tumor.

Aim of the Project

Objective: Detect and classify skin cancer using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) with an aim to achieve 90% accuracy.

Significance: Early detection of skin cancer can significantly increase the survival rate. However, the early detection process is challenging and expensive.

Problem Definition

Overview: Skin cancer is the most prevalent cancer in the United States. Overexposure to UV light from the sun or tanning beds is the most preventable cause.

Current Techniques: Existing imaging techniques provide detailed anatomical delineation and are essential in identifying that neurological symptoms result from brain tumors.

Methodology-Convolutional Neural Network (CNN):

Definition: A CNN is a type of neural network with one or more convolutional layers, mainly used for image processing, classification, and segmentation.

Functionality: A convolution slides a filter over the input. Traditional neural networks aren't ideal for image processing as they require images in reduced-resolution pieces.

Advantage: CNNs have neurons arranged like the frontal lobe, responsible for processing visual stimuli, allowing them to cover the entire visual field and avoid the piecemeal processing issue of traditional neural networks.
